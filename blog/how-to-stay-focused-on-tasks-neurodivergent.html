How to Stay Focused on Tasks When Your Brain Keeps Wandering

ADHD Jul 18, 2026

Focus is not something you force. Focus is something you design for.

This is especially true for neurodivergent people. ADHD can make it harder to stay on task, manage distractions, and complete long tasks unless they are interesting or externally supported.

So instead of asking, "How do I force myself to focus?" ask: "How can I make returning to the task easier?"

Reduce visible distractions

Your environment is always giving your brain options.

Open tabs, dishes, notifications, laundry, Slack messages, and random papers are all invitations to switch attention.

Try this before a focus block: Close unrelated tabs. Put your phone across the room. Clear only the surface you need. Keep one "later" note open for distracting thoughts.

You do not need a perfect environment. You need fewer competing signals.

Use one task surface

One common reason focus breaks is that the task is spread across too many places.

The notes are in one app. The deadline is in another. The file is in another. The checklist is in your head.

Bring the task into one visible surface. That might be one document, one Notion page, one checklist, or one timer next to one next action.

Use timers kindly

Timers should not create panic. They should create boundaries.

Try 10 minutes for a hard start, 20 minutes for admin, 25 minutes for focused work, and 5 minutes for cleanup.

For time blindness, a visual timer can be more useful than a clock because it shows time passing instead of simply telling the time. Time-management difficulty is often discussed in connection with ADHD and executive function challenges.

Create a distraction protocol

When a distraction appears, do not argue with it.

Use this: Capture the distracting thought. Return to the exact next action. Restart the timer. Reward the return.

The skill is not "never getting distracted." The skill is returning.

A focus reset script

When attention breaks, say: "What was I doing?" "What is the next visible action?" "Can I do 10 minutes?" "What distraction needs to be captured?"

You are allowed to restart 20 times.
